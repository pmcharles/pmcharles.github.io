Scientific Computation PhD Student

University of Maryland, College Park

Phil Charles

I am a sixth-year Scientific Computation PhD candidate at the University of Maryland, College Park. My main research interests and experience lie at the intersection of numerical PDE solvers and deep learning.

In my doctoral research, I have implemented deep learning into numerical solvers for PDEs through two approaches:

  1. The deep learning model is incorporated in a less intrusive manner. To this end, consider a numerical PDE solver that is mathematically sound. Now, if there is some component that has a bottleneck (e.g. expensive or constrained algorithm), it is possible to repace it with a suitable neural network.
  2. The deep learning model itself serves as the full PDE solver (as in a surrogate model in operator learning). However, it is important to embed the structure of the PDE (e.g. the variational form of the PDE) into the model's architecture to make the deep-learned model more interpretable.
